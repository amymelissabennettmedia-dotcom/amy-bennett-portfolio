Meet Amy

I move between human needs, system behavior, and implementation details to shape experiences that are clear, intuitive, and usable.

I’m interested in how structure influences clarity: how interaction patterns emerge, where friction develops, and how systems can be shaped to feel more intuitive, adaptive, and human.

I design across UX, interaction systems, and human-AI interaction systems, moving between conceptual architecture, front-end implementation, and iterative refinement depending on what the system requires.

Rather than approaching design as isolated screens or visual outputs, I treat it as an evolving relationship between people, context, communication, and behavior.
